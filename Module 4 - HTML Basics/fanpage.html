Linkin Park

Linkin Park is an iconic American rock band from California. They became famous for combining nu metal and rap metal and brought it to the forefront of pop music. Their most successful albums were Hybrid Theory (2000) and Meteora (2003).

Bio

How It All Started

The band was originally formed by three high school friends: Rob Bourdon, Brad Delson and Mike Shinoda.

In March 1999, Linkin Park as we know it came to be when Chester Bennington was recommended to join Rob Bourdon, Brad Delson, Joe Hahn and Mike Shinoda. The band was originally names Xero and first changed to Hybrid Theory after Chester joined. The band later settled on Linkin Park as homage to Lincoln Park in Santa Monica which Chester used to drive past regularly. The band used Linkin after being unable to acquire the internet domain "lincolnpark.com".

Legacy

Linkin Park is one of the best selling alternative rock bands of the 21st Century, having sold more than 70 million albums worldwide and winning 2 Grammy Awards. In 2012, the band was voted as the greatest artist of the 2000s in a Bracket Madness poll on VH1.

Since Chester's suicide in 2017, the band has gone into indefinite hiatus.
